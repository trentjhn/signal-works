# Project Intelligence — SignalWorks

## What this is
A single-page marketing website for SignalWorks, an AI automation consulting business, that establishes credibility and routes potential clients to a $500 discovery call booking.

## Target audience
Small business owners and marketing agency principals, primarily in LA and Bay Area. Tech-aware but not developers. They subscribe to ChatGPT and use some AI tools but don't know how to automate their actual workflows. Losing time and money to repetitive manual work. The prototype client: a 50s marketing agency owner with a network of similar business owners who wants to "become AI native" but doesn't know where to start.

## Primary goal
One action: book the AI Opportunity Audit (Calendly link). Everything on the page serves this CTA or builds credibility toward it. There is no secondary goal.

## Brand personality
**Precise. Warm. Credible.** Boutique strategy firm energy, not tech startup energy. The site should feel like it was built by people who do serious work — not people who want to seem impressive. Think editorial warmth over SaaS blue. McKinsey clarity, not Product Hunt launch energy.

## What this is NOT
- Not a tech startup site (no purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no "AI-powered" x15)
- Not a corporate consulting site (no stock imagery, no vague mission statements)
- Not a personal portfolio (it's a business, not a resume)
- Not generic (every word and pixel should be specific to SignalWorks)

## Copy brief

### Voice and tone
Direct, warm, Oakland-rooted, specific. Not corporate. Not startup hype. Sounds like a knowledgeable person talking, not a pitch deck.

### Audience's internal monologue
"I know AI is supposed to help my business but I have no idea where to start. I've tried ChatGPT but it doesn't actually save me time. My team is still doing the same repetitive stuff. I need someone who actually builds this, not another consultant who talks about it."

### The one thing they need to believe
"These people build real things at real scale, and they can apply that to my actual workflows."

### Copy constraints
- No "revolutionize," "transform," "unlock," "unleash," "leverage," "seamlessly," "effortlessly"
- No "AI-powered" more than once (or zero times) on the page
- Claims reference specific results (140M users, 52% lift, $2.4M margin)
- CTAs are verb + specific outcome ("Book the Audit" not "Get Started")
- US English, conversational professional tone
- First person plural ("we") for the brand voice

### Problem
**Label:** THE PROBLEM
**Headline:** You're subscribed to the tools. The manual work didn't stop.
**Body:** Most small businesses and agencies are paying employees to do things that could run automatically — moving data between apps, following up on leads, summarizing reports, updating CRMs. Not because the solutions don't exist. Because nobody on your team has the time or background to connect the pieces. The AI tools are there. The workflow is still broken.
**Pain point callouts:**
- Client reporting that takes hours to compile
- Follow-up emails that fall through the cracks
- Staff copy-pasting between tools every morning
- Proposals built from scratch every time

### Process
**Label:** HOW IT WORKS
**Headline:** Three steps. First one tells you everything you need to know.

**Step 1: The Audit** — We sit down with you and go through your actual day-to-day — what tools you use, where time gets lost, what your team does that a system could handle instead. Most clients are surprised how quickly the high-impact opportunities surface. You leave with a written roadmap and a ranked list of what's worth building. No vague findings.

**Step 2: We Build** — If you want to move forward, we build the automations that came out of the audit. We connect your tools, configure the logic, and test against real scenarios. You don't need to be technical. You just need to show us your workflows. We hand off working automations, not mockups.

**Step 3: Hand Off and Run** — We hand off with documentation and a walkthrough video, so your team knows exactly what was built and how to use it. If you want us to stay on for ongoing maintenance and new automations as your needs change, we can. If you want to take it from here, you have everything you need.

**Closing line:** Most clients have their first automation running not long after the audit.

**Copy direction (2026-04-14):**
